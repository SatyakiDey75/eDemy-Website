What Makes This Career Worth Pursuing?

Do you want to help children who have trouble learning at a regular pace in classes? Then, being a special educator can be a great career path for you! We have all watched the movie ‘Taare Zameen Par’. There are a lot of kids out there with such disabilities, an inability to read, write or even understand. If you feel that you want to help by teaching them, then being a special educator might be the right task for you. It involves helping such children grow, and overcome their troubles.

Learning disabilities (such as dyslexia), communication disorders, emotional and behavioral disorders (such as ADHD), physical disabilities (such as osteogenesis imperfecta, cerebral palsy, muscular dystrophy, spina bifida, and Friedreich's ataxia), and developmental disabilities (such as autistic spectrum disorders) are all addressed in special education.

Description

A career in special education is for those who have an incredible passion for teaching and attachment and concern for children with special needs. Special education means personalised schooling given to children with different disabilities both physical and mental. Children with such disabilities need special training and instruction by well-trained professionals in the field of special education. Special education offers different career opportunities including teaching, instruction and curriculum development for children with special needs and adults. The major responsibility of any special educator is to teach and interact with children who have a variety of disabilities.

Skills You Need

  • Communication Skills: A teacher’s primary responsibility is to impart knowledge to their students, making the ability to effectively communicate very crucial.
  • Compassion Skills: One needs to be very empathetic to the needs of their students and show care for them.
  • Creativity Skills: One may need to devise creative ways to impart education to increase the amount of what the students understand and retain.
  • Organization Skills: As one is responsible for the creation of various modules, they need to show organisational ability.

How To Pursue

  • School: Any subject combination taking Science or Commerce or Humanities.
  • Graduation: Bachelor course in any field / Bachelor of Elementary Education / Bachelor of Education in Special Education
  • Post Graduation: Post Graduate Diploma in Special Education

Job Opportunities

  • Special Educator for Early/ Pre-KG to Class 12
  • Special Educator in College/ University
  • Special Educator for Low-Incidence Disabilities/ Sensory Development
  • Special Educator for High-Incidence Disabilities
  • Special Educator for Emotional or Behavioural Disorders
  • Special Educator for Autism Spectrum Disorders

Top Indian Institutes

  • B.P. Marine Academy

  • Netaji Subhas National Institute of Sports

  • Institution Of Mechanical Engineers

Pros

  • The hours for a special educator are chosen by the teachers themselves.
  • There’s a lot of freedom for these teachers.
  • You get a satisfaction when the children you teach grow to be successful.

Cons

  • One needs to have a lot of patience to deal with some children.
  • The salary paid to special educators is quite less.
  • There is less scope for promotion.
