    What Makes This Career Worth Pursuing?

    Do you think your heart is a gem and you can devote to the service of your society? Then, being a counsellor is the right career path for you. A counsellor understands the troubles of their clients, emotional, mental, psychological, career oriented etc. and helps them solve it. Their job is to provide guidance to people who require it, particularly on personal issues. Children who have suffered in this way should seek help from a bereavement counselor.

    Professional counseling is a type of professional interaction that helps individuals, families, and groups achieve their mental health, wellness, education, and career goals. Counselors operate in a number of community settings to provide counseling, rehabilitation, and support services to people. Their responsibilities differ significantly based on their expertise, which is defined by the environment in which they work.

    Description

    A counsellor gets to know people and solve any trouble they’re having mentally. They’re supposed to build trust with their clients. They help them resolve their feelings. They let people rise above themselves and become better versions of themselves. They let people watch things from a new perspective and let them heal their minds. They have to assist clients in reaching their optimal level of psychosocial functioning through resolving negative patterns. They help them in rehabilitation and improving their quality of life. Counsellors must be aware of these difficulties in order to provide proper therapy and assistance to their clients.

    Skills You Need

    • Communication Skills: To effectively communicate with a variety of people, you must have great verbal communication abilities. Counselors will frequently be asked to question clients and conduct interviews, and they must be skilled in doing so so as not to upset or stress clients further.
    • Interpersonal Skills: Counselors must be able to communicate successfully with a variety of people, including clients and coworkers.
    • Compassion Skills: You should want to help people, be compassionate, and be able to empathize with their sorrow and other issues. You must be able to put your patients at ease and make them feel at ease in your presence.

    How To Pursue

    • School: Any subject combination taking Science or Commerce or Humanities.
    • Graduation: Bachelor of Science / Bachelor of Arts in Psychology or Sociology.
    • Post Graduation: Masters of Science / Masters of Arts in Psychology or Sociology.
