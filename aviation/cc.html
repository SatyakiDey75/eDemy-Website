    What Makes This Career Worth Pursuing?

    Do you love travelling and interacting and helping people? If so, then a career as a part of the Cabin Crew is just for you. Cabin Crew members travel on flights to ensure that the journey of all people in the aeroplane is comfortable and without any problem. They are also responsible for ensuring everyone’s safety and lead in times of emergency.

    They are also supposed to fill out the flight reports and do the necessary paperwork for the flight. They also have to greet passengers as they board and exit the plane. show passengers to their seats, serve meals and refreshments, check the condition and provision of emergency equipment and information for passengers. Flight attendants are prepared to handle a wide range of emergencies and are first-aid certified.

    Description

    Being a part of the Cabin Crew is of great responsibility. The crew is responsible for checking all of the safety measures and look into the passenger list and facilitate the special requirements for some passengers. They also help passengers with any difficulty they might have during the flight and serve them refreshments. They are responsible for some of the important work to ensure that the passengers have a great experience of travelling. They have to answer queries and provide assistance to passengers to ensure their pleasure. Before taking off, they inspect the cabin for conformity with safety rules.

    Skills You Need

    • MInterpersonal Skills: This profession requires you to interact with multitudes of people regularly, making good interpersonal skills important.
    • Pressure Handling Skills: You have to be ready to act efficiently and smartly under emergencies despite all of the pressure that may come upon you.
    • Teamwork Skills: You work in teams of crews, making teamwork very important.

    How To Pursue

    • School: Any subject combination taking Science or Commerce or Humanities.
    • Graduation: Diploma in Cabin Crew / Bachelor in Hotel Management Catering and Technology.
    • Post Graduation: PG Diploma in Cabin Crew.

    Pros

    • You get to interact with a huge array of people.
    • You may also get discounted or free flights as per the perks of the job.
    • You get to develop your personality.

    Cons

    • The working hours are long and exhausting.
    • You may also have to encounter a few passengers who are difficult to handle but still be pleasing to them.
    • You may sometimes be deprived of sleep in cases of night flights, about the latest improvements.
