    What Makes This Career Worth Pursuing?

    Do you aspire to fly high? If yes then, the flight attendant is the perfect job for you. A flight attendant is a member of an aircrew employed by airlines aboard commercial flights, primarily to ensure the comfort of passengers.

    Their job is not just to serve people snacks and drinks but flight attendants are also in charge of the safety of the passengers onboard. Flight attendants are also responsible for briefing the passengers on safety standards specific to the aircraft in a safety demonstration. Passengers are made aware of how to locate their nearest emergency exit, how to properly buckle their safety belts, what to do in the event of turbulence, how to operate safety vests or flotation devices, and how to use the drop-down oxygen masks.

    Description

    The job of the flight attendant begins way before the flight takes off and ends with the landing of the flight. It begins with a briefing about the weather conditions, journey time, possible emergency situations, etc. The primary job of flight attendants is to keep passengers safe and to ensure that everyone follows security regulations. Along with that, they also serve and sometimes sell beverages, meals, or snacks or other items available on flight. Working hours of a flight attendant are not fixed; instead, it depends on the flight schedule and duration.

    How To Pursue

    • School: Any subject combination taking Science or Commerce or Humanities.
    • Graduation: Diploma in Cabin Crew / Bachelor in Hotel Management Catering and Technology.
    • Post Graduation: PG Diploma in Cabin Crew.

    Pros

    • It is a great opportunity to meet new people and to travel to different locations.
    • One does not need any specific degree to become a flight attendant.
    • The pay is satisfactory and it increases every year, alongwith free flights and hotel discounts for the attendants and their family.

    Cons

    • Seniority gets picked first every time, whether in the promotion or in the recommendation.
    • There is a tough competition to get into this field as companies receive thousands of applications.
    • Inconsistency in schedule makes the attendants work even on holidays.
