    What Makes This Career Worth Pursuing?

    Do you want to be a digital expert and influence the brands? If yes, then you should consider UI/UX Design as a career option. The design of user interfaces for equipment and software, such as computers, home appliances, mobile devices, and other electronic devices, to optimize usability and the user experience, is known as user interface design or user interface engineering.

    User interface design (UI) is the design of user interfaces used for machines and software with the focus on maximizing usability and the User Experience (UX). The goal of user interface design is to make the user's interaction as simple and efficient as possible, in terms of accomplishing goals. UI/UX Designers have to work on various software’s. They have to design facilitators and conductors who rapidly conduct research.

    Description

    UI/UX Designing is a technical job. The job of a UI/UX designer is to design all the screens through which a user will move and to create the visual elements—and their interactive properties—that facilitate this movement. Designers are responsible for the technical aspect of the product. Their work attracts customers and they make products convenient for the users. They do not work on the product as a physical entity instead, they work on the websites which help the product to sell and build its brand name. A UI/UX Designer requires specialized training as this position needs the elements of programming, psychology and digital design.

    Skills You Need

    • Communication Skills: Along with vocal communication, designers need to communicate visually. As a storyteller, they have to be convincing to make customers buy products.
    • Software Skills: UI/UX Designers have to work on various software’s. They have to design facilitators and conductors who rapidly conduct research.
    • Reading and Writing Skills: Majority of the work is done theoretically to eliminate huge losses of resources.
    • Business Skills: Designers help to build a business and in need to do so, they should themselves have business skills.
    • Revising Skills: Designers do not always build from scratch instead; the majority of their time is spent in improving the previous designs using the feedback.
