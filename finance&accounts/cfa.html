    What Makes This Career Worth Pursuing?

    Do you have a keen interest in finance and its technicalities? Then CFA might be the perfect role for you! The Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA) Program is a postgraduate professional certification provided to investment and financial professionals worldwide by the American-based CFA Institute (formerly the Association for Investment Management and Research, or AIMR). Candidates must pass three levels of exams. These include topics like accounting, economics, ethics, financial management, and security analysis.

    The CFA charter is generally regarded as the gold standard in the field of investment research and is one of the most prestigious designations in finance. The CFA designation is meant to show that you have a solid foundation in advanced investment analysis and portfolio management, as well as a strong commitment to ethical behaviour.

    Description

    Any work that involves large-scale wealth management, financial analysis, or complex accounting is likely to be held by a Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA). A financial analyst is in charge of collecting data, arranging information, analyzing historical results, forecasting and projecting future results, making suggestions, and creating Excel models and presentations. Depending on the company they work for, the sector they work in, and their level of seniority, analysts have a wide range of duties and responsibilities. The role encompasses relationship and asset management, credit analysis, trading, accounting and auditing, financial planning, etc.

    Skills You Need

    • Excel Modelling Skills: CFA charter holders deal with a lot of Excel sheets and templates.
    • Analytical Skills: They can visualize, articulate, conceptualize, or solve both complicated and basic problems by making rational decisions based on the information they have.
    • Communication Skills: They must interact with a variety of people, including customers, investors, stockbrokers, and others.
    • Computer Skills: To build portfolios, analyze financial data, spot patterns, and make predictions, they will need to learn new technologies.
    • Mathematics Skills: When calculating the value of financial instruments, the CFA classification employs mathematical skills.

    How To Pursue

    • School: Any subject combination taking Commerce as the main stream.
    • Graduation: Bachelor of Commerce in Finance.
    • Post Graduation: CFA Program.
