    What Makes This Career Worth Pursuing?

    Are you interested in numbers and finances? Then you can consider accountancy as a career! Auditing, accounting, administrative, and general management are all areas of business and finance where chartered accountants work. Some work in public practice, while others work in the private sector, and still others work for government agencies.

    The CA (Chartered Accountant) is a prestigious and demanding profession. The ICAI (Institute of Chartered Accountants of India) runs the chartered accountant program in India (Indian Chartered Accountants Institute). They are members of the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI). The membership may be renewed by paying the prescribed fees or paying an annual charge.

    Description

    Chartered accountants manage financial systems and budgets, and undertake financial audits. They liaise with clients and provide financial information and advice. They advise clients on business transactions, such as mergers and acquisitions and also counsel clients on areas of business improvement, or dealing with insolvency. They help to detect and prevent fraud. They prepare financial statements, including monthly and annual accounts arranging financial management reports, including financial planning and forecasting. They advise on tax and treasury issues and also negotiate terms with suppliers.

    Skills You Need

    • Mathematical Skills: Accuracy and speed in general mathematics help hugely.
    • Analytical Skills: They obtain technical qualifications and improve analytical skills when working on practical examples during their articles.
    • Communication Skills: During the internship, the applicant may interact with clients from various industries and provide customized services tailored to their needs.

    How To Pursue

    • School: Any subject combination taking Commerce as the main stream.
    • Graduation: Bachelor of Commerce in Finance.
    • Post Graduation: Master of Commerce / Master of Business Administration.

    Job Opportunities

    • Public/Private Practice
    • Government Organizations
    • Banking and Insurance Sector
    • Corporate Finance
    • NGOs
    • Law Firms

    Top Indian Institutes

    • Institute of Chartered Accountants of India

    Pros

    • Good salary as they deal with many clients simultaneously.
    • Social status as this is a respectable job.
    • High demand as the business culture is growing.

    Cons

    • Requires hard work as the exams are difficult to pass.
    • Long working hours especially during the end of the financial year.
    • Takes time to build a career as experience is required.
