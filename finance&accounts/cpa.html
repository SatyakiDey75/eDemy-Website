    What Makes This Career Worth Pursuing?

    Are you intrigued by accountancy and similar fields? Then, CPA can be a great profession for you! Certified public accountants provide their services to public organizations. If you think you have a flair for accounts and want to serve the community, for the betterment of the community, then the work of a CPA might be the best job for you.

    Certified Public Accountant is a designation given to certified accountants in many English-speaking countries. In other English-speaking nations, it is roughly equal to the title of a chartered accountant. The CPA is a license that allows you to conduct public accounting services in the United States. State licensing standards vary, but the Uniform Certified Public Accountant Examination, 150 semester units of college education, and one year of accounting-related work are the minimum requirements.

    Description

    Certified Public Accountant or CPA provides accounting services to the public and organizations. They are primarily involved in auditing and attestation procedures. They should be well versed with accounting principles, business and financial concepts, tax, etc. CPAs are hired by organizations in the role of finance manager, financial analyst, chief financial officer, etc. They are usually hired by NGOs. As needed, organizing and updating accounting records (digital and physical) Creating and evaluating transaction reports. Conducting rigorous audits on a regular basis to check the accuracy of financial documentation, expenditures, and investments.

    Skills You Need

    • Excel Modelling Skills: CPAs have to deal with a lot of Excel sheets and templates.
    • Analytical Skills: They can visualize, articulate, conceptualize, or solve both complicated and basic problems by making rational decisions based on the information they have.
    • Communication Skills: They must interact with a variety of people, including customers, investors, stockbrokers, and others.
    • Mathematical Skills: When calculating the value of financial instruments, they need to employ mathematical skills.

    How To Pursue

    • School: Any subject combination taking Commerce as the main stream.
    • Graduation: Bachelor of Commerce in Finance / Bachelor of Commerce in (Hons.) / Bachelor of Business Administration.
    • Post Graduation: Master of Business Administration in Finance / Chartered Accountant / Company Secretary.
