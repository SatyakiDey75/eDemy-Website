    What Makes This Career Worth Pursuing?

    Want to contribute to the nation's economic development? Then, an alluring career is awaiting you. A service introduced by Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru for formulating and implementing economic policies and programs in India is one of the prestigious job opportunities of the Central Civil Services of the executive branch of the Government of India categorized under Group A.

    The Engineering Services Examination is conducted by the Union Public Service Commission to recruit officers for the services which cater to the technical functions of the Government of India. It is a three-stage competitive examination comprising objective, subjective and personality tests. After being selected, they are assigned to the position of Assistant Executive Engineer or Assistant Director.

    Description

    IES officers' roles can be categorized in terms of economic administration, implementation of various policies of development, working for the sake of economic reforms followed by price fixation, evaluation and monitoring. In addition to purely economic services, these officials make a critical contribution to policy development in social sectors. They would provide ongoing advice to the relevant ministries/ departments on all the policies influencing domestic and external economic management and facilitating reforms in the relevant fields. Roads, railways, manufacturing, defence, construction, telecommunications, and other technical fields are all covered by an IES Officer.

    Skills You Need

    • Analytical Skills: As an Economist your foremost job is to analyse the patterns of activities and review them, making analytical skills very important.
    • Critical Thinking Skills: One needs to be able to assess various factors critically and go in deep depths to draw fruitful conclusions, making this skill very important.
    • Communication Skills: In this field, you are responsible for being the bridge between ideas and their implementation, making communication skills very important.

    How To Pursue

    • School: Any subject combination taking Science or Commerce or Humanities.
    • Graduation: Bachelor's in any stream
    • Post Graduation: UPSC Entrance Exams.
