    What Makes This Career Worth Pursuing?

    Are you fascinated by the subject of chemistry and love to learn about various phenomena? If yes, then you should think of making a career in chemistry. Chemistry is the scientific field that involves elements and compounds composed of atoms, molecules and ions. This field aims to study their composition, structure, properties, behaviour and the changes they undergo during a reaction with other substances.

    Chemistry is everywhere, even our bodies are made up of chemical compounds. Our daily life activities are also a result of chemical reactions like cooking, fermenting milk to form a curd, lighting matches, making ice, etc. Chemistry helps us to understand our surroundings better by providing us with reasons.

    Description

    Chemists are required to be creative, careful, and productive, furthermore, they need to think about the future. Chemistry is an academic field that requires experts to observe the basic properties of materials, conduct experiments and test their experimental limits. They do this to find the matter’s usage and application to make a product. Their duties involve modelling, analysis, synthesis, research, limited fieldwork, or even sales and information management. They measure proportions and reaction rates to understand unfamiliar substances and how they behave, or to create new compounds for use in a variety of practical applications.

    Skills You Need

    • Reading Skills: Professionals have to deal with a lot of documents so regular reading becomes an efficient skill.
    • Critical Thinking Skills: Professionals need to use logic and reasoning to find solutions and to reach conclusions.
    • Problem Solving Skills: Candidates should be able to identify complex problems and review related information to develop and evaluate options and implement solutions.
    • Technical Skills: Being a chemist is an academic field thus, the clear concepts of science and the passion for science is required..

    How To Pursue

    • School: Any subject combination taking Science as the main stream.
    • Graduation: Bachelor of Science Honours in Chemistry or Biochemistry.
    • Post Graduation: Masters of Science in Chemistry / Post Graduate Diploma in Chemistry or Biochemistry or Chemical Engineering
