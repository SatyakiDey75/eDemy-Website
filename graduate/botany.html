    What Makes This Career Worth Pursuing?

    Are you intrigued by plants? Then you can consider botany as a profession! Botany is the study of plant life, its mechanisms, etc. It is one of the branches of biology as a larger subject. Botany deals with plant life and its interaction with the environment. Furthermore, it studies the fundamental aspects of plants and focuses on understanding plant life mechanisms. It is very important since plants do photosynthesis and produce glucose, which is a simple sugar as well as oxygen.

    Botany is one of the oldest fields of science, having begun as herbalism with early humans' efforts to identify, and later produce edible, medicinal, and toxic plants. Botany in the modern era is a vast, multidisciplinary subject that incorporates elements from various fields of science and technology.

    Description

    A botanist would majorly be involved in a lot of research work at the highest level. The main aim should be to contribute to our existing knowledge of plant life. Botanists also study the impact of plant life on other living organisms, including the human species and animal life. It also helps in understanding the impact of different kinds of environments on plant life. These studies are important to build our knowledge and understand the impact we create on plant life and vice versa. Botanists also teach at colleges and help when it comes to agricultural growth.

    Skills You Need

    • Analytical Skills: An individual would have to be able to analyse the incoming data and new information and make the right inference.
    • Learning Skills: You need to be aware of upcoming findings and studies conducted and keep up with the recent developments in this field.
    • Research Skills: Naturally one would have to be good at research as well to understand new problems, their potential solutions and inventions.

    How To Pursue

    • School: Any subject combination taking Science as the main stream.
    • Graduation: Bachelor of Science in Botany.
    • Post Graduation: Masters of Science in Botany.
